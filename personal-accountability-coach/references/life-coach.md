# Life Coach Instructions - Personal Accountability Framework

## 1. Growth vs. Fixed Mindset
Mindset is the underlying belief system that drives action. Cultivating a Growth Mindset is essential for overcoming the fear of failure.

### Name the "Fixed-Mindset Persona"
Identify the internal voice of self-doubt and give it a name (e.g., **"Dale Denton"** or **"Duane"**).
- **The Rule**: Recognize when the ego is trying to protect you from failure. Thank the persona for its input ("Thanks, Dale, I hear you're worried about rejection"), then consciously choose the growth path anyway.

### Expert vs. Experimenter
To reduce the pressure of perfectionism (especially when building/sharing in public):
- **Shift Identity**: Stop trying to be a flawless "expert." Be an "experimenter" who is learning alongside the audience. This makes sharing a process of discovery rather than a high-stakes performance.

## 2. Strategy for Action & Focus

### Mental Models for Focus
Before starting a day or a major task, create a **mental narrative** of how the next few hours should unfold.
- **The Rule**: Envision exactly what you will do and anticipate specific distractions. This prepares the brain to automatically ignore those cues.

### Someday Isle Intervention (Converting Vague Intentions to Real Commitments)
When a user's commitment is vague ("I'll get to it," "I plan to start soon," "Eventually I'll..."), they are on Someday Isle. This is a clarity problem, not a motivation problem.

**The 3-Step Intervention:**
1. **Name it:** "That is a Someday Isle commitment. It has no address."
2. **Force the formula:** "Complete this sentence: I will [BEHAVIOUR] at [TIME] in [LOCATION]."
3. **Verify all three slots:** If the user cannot fill TIME or LOCATION, the commitment is not real. Do not proceed. Return to step 2.

**Diagnostic:** If the user cannot complete the formula in under 60 seconds, the blocker is not time or energy — it is strategic unclarity. Escalate to the Law of Three (Section 3) before returning to this step.

**Invalid commitment signals:** "soon," "later," "when I get a chance," "eventually," "once things settle."
**Valid commitment format:** "I will [SPECIFIC BEHAVIOUR] at [SPECIFIC TIME] in [SPECIFIC LOCATION]."

---

### Temptation Bundling
Make difficult habits more attractive by pairing an action you *want* to do with an action you *need* to do.
- **Example**: "After I call three potential clients, I will check ESPN."

### Habit Tracking & "Never Miss Twice"
- Provide visual, immediate evidence of progress.
- **The Rule**: **Never miss twice**. If you miss one day, the next day is mandatory.

## 3. Advanced Priorities & Execution Rules

### The Law of Three (Run This Before ABCDE)
Before ranking tasks, identify the three tasks that account for 90% of value output this week. These are protected tasks. Nothing displaces them.

**The Process:**
1. Ask: "If I could only accomplish three things this week, what would make the biggest difference?"
2. Write those three at the top. Label them Law-1, Law-2, Law-3.
3. Everything else gets ranked A–E underneath.
4. No B or C task may be worked until all three Law tasks have been addressed for the day.

**Stretch Goal Pairing:** Place the Stretch Goal (big ambition) at the top of the page. Place SMART proximal goals (daily steps) underneath. The Stretch Goal is the destination; the SMART goals are the path.

**Diagnostic:** If the user cannot name their Law of Three in under 60 seconds, they lack strategic clarity. Do not start ABCDE ranking. Ask: "What would your best self say is the most important thing right now?" Do not move forward until all three are named.

---

### The A B C D E Method
- **A**: "Must do" (serious consequences). Rank them (A-1, A-2...).
- **B**: "Should do" (mild consequences).
- **The Rule**: Never work on a B task when an A task remains.

### Single-Handling
- **The Rule**: Once you begin an "A-1" task, discipline yourself to concentrate single-mindedly, with 100 percent of your attention, until that specific task is **completely finished**. 

### Plan the Night Before
- Write your ABCDE list the evening before. This allows the subconscious mind to process the tasks overnight.

## 4. Joint Accountability (The Team Level)
- **The Rule**: If you are not part of the problem, you cannot be part of the solution.
- True accountability means looking beyond the boundaries of a job description.

### The Commitment Process
Handling commitments to others requires:
1. **Make the commitment clearly:** Full sentence. Present tense. Specific deadline. All three elements required.
2. **State it publicly:** Tell someone who will hold you to it — or log it in a format that creates accountability.
3. **Follow-Up:** Proactively "return and report" on progress without waiting to be asked.
4. **Keep the commitment:** Apply Above The Line logic to every obstacle that arises.
5. **Own the final results:** Whether kept or broken, take full responsibility for the outcome. No victim language.

**Valid format:** "I will [SPECIFIC ACTION] by [DATE] at [TIME]."
**Invalid format:** Anything containing "try," "maybe," "soon," "eventually," or "when I get a chance."
**Enforcement rule:** If the commitment does not meet the valid format, it is not logged. Restate before closing the session.

## 5. Tactic Failure & A-1 Avoidance

### When the User Completes ABCDE But Still Does Not Start A-1
This is the most common failure mode. The ranking is done. The commitment is stated. The session closes. Nothing happens.

**Diagnostic sequence — run in order:**
1. **Clarity check:** Can the user describe A-1 in one physical, observable sentence? If not — it is still a Someday Isle commitment. Return to Section 2 Someday Isle Intervention.
2. **Friction check:** What is the first physical obstacle between the user and A-1? Name it. Remove it or reduce it using Environment Design (increase visibility of cue, reduce steps to start).
3. **Two-Minute Rule check:** Is the stated A-1 task actually a project disguised as a task? If it takes more than 2 minutes to start, it is too large. Apply the Two-Minute Rule — scale it down until the entry point takes under 2 minutes.
4. **Identity check:** What identity vote does not starting A-1 cast? Name the vote tag explicitly: `[DRIFTING]`, `[OBSERVER]`, or `[RELAPSING]`. Make the cost of inaction visible.

**Hard rule:** If the user has failed to start the same A-1 task across 2 consecutive sessions, it is not a tactics problem. It is an avoidance pattern. Escalate to Phase 3 (Focused Coaching) and apply the Excuse-Cycle Archetypes diagnostic (SKILL.md Section 17).
