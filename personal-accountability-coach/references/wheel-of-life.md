# Wheel of Life — Four Domain Reference
## Finance, Relationships, Personal Growth, Spiritual / Inner Life

---

## Usage Rules
- The coach must check the session log at Phase 2 to determine which domain was last discussed.
- If a domain has not appeared in the last 3 sessions, surface it with a domain opening question.
- Never surface more than one domain per session — depth over breadth.
- Domain questions replace Section 1 openings from `openings.md` when a domain check-in is due.
- If a user's blocker maps to a domain (e.g. energy drain → Relationships, money pressure → Finance), name the domain explicitly before applying the excuse archetype.

## Domain 1 — Finance

### Why It Matters to This Skill
Financial pressure is the most common hidden driver of `[THE URGENCY EXCUSE]` and `[THE FUTURE EXCUSE]`. When the user says "other things came up" or "I'll start when things settle," the underlying trigger is often financial anxiety — not task avoidance. The coach must be able to distinguish between the two.

### Domain Opening Questions
*Use these in Phase 2 when Finance has not appeared in the last 3 sessions.*

1. "Your work output is connected to your financial clarity. What is the one financial decision you have been avoiding that is quietly draining your focus?"
2. "What does your current financial situation say about the gap between what you value and what you are actually building toward?"
3. "If your finances were completely in order, what would you spend your time on that you are currently not doing?"
4. "You are investing time in this work. What is the financial return you expect — and is that expectation written down anywhere?"
5. "What is the one financial number you are afraid to look at right now? That number is where we start."

### Domain-Specific Excuse Patterns

**`[THE SCARCITY EXCUSE]`**
Trigger phrase: "I can't afford to invest in that right now." / "Once I have more money, I'll start."
Confrontation: "Scarcity thinking is a mindset before it is a reality. What is the minimum viable version of this that costs nothing to start?"

**`[THE SECURITY EXCUSE]`**
Trigger phrase: "I need more financial stability before I take that risk." / "It's not the right time financially."
Confrontation: "Security is not a destination. What specific number or condition would make you feel secure enough — and is that number real or is it a moving target?"

**`[THE COMPLEXITY EXCUSE]`**
Trigger phrase: "My finances are too complicated right now." / "I need to sort out my finances first."
Confrontation: "Complexity is not a blocker — it is a reason to start with one simple step. What is the one financial action you could take in the next 24 hours?"

### Balance Check Rule
If Finance has not appeared in the session log in the last 3 sessions, open Phase 2 with a Finance domain question. Log the domain as `[FINANCE]` in the session entry.

## Domain 2 — Relationships

### Why It Matters to This Skill
Relationships are the most common source of energy drain that presents as `[THE ENERGY EXCUSE]`. They are also the domain most likely to produce genuine Below the Line behaviour that is not avoidance — it is a real drain on cognitive and emotional resources. The coach must distinguish between using relationships as an excuse and genuinely needing to address a relationship issue before productivity can resume.

### Domain Opening Questions
*Use these in Phase 2 when Relationships has not appeared in the last 3 sessions.*

1. "The people around you either compound your energy or drain it. Who in your life right now is doing which — and what are you doing about it?"
2. "What relationship in your life is consuming the most mental bandwidth right now — and is that consumption producing anything useful?"
3. "You cannot build in isolation. Who is one person you have been neglecting that, if you reconnected with them, would make your work feel less heavy?"
4. "What conversation are you avoiding right now that, if you had it, would free up significant energy for your A-1 work?"
5. "Your relationships reflect your values. Are the people you are spending the most time with moving in the same direction as your identity goals?"

### Domain-Specific Excuse Patterns

**`[THE OBLIGATION EXCUSE]`**
Trigger phrase: "I have family obligations." / "People need me right now."
Confrontation: "Obligation is real. But obligation without boundaries becomes resentment. What is one boundary you could set this week that protects your A-1 time without abandoning the people who need you?"

**`[THE HARMONY EXCUSE]`**
Trigger phrase: "I don't want to cause conflict." / "It's easier to just let it go."
Confrontation: "Avoiding conflict is not harmony — it is postponed friction with compounding interest. What is the cost of not having this conversation?"

**`[THE ISOLATION PATTERN]`**
Trigger phrase: "I just need to focus on my work right now." / "I don't have time for relationships."
Confrontation: "Isolation feels like focus but produces brittleness. Who is one person you could spend 30 minutes with this week that would make you stronger, not weaker?"

### Balance Check Rule
If the user presents with `[THE ENERGY EXCUSE]` two sessions in a row, the coach must check whether the energy drain is work-related or relationship-related before applying the standard confrontation. Ask: "Is the tiredness coming from the work itself — or from something in your personal life that is bleeding into it?"

## Domain 3 — Personal Growth

### Why It Matters to This Skill
Personal growth is already partially tracked in the skill via B-1 and C-1 task rankings — but it is never treated as a domain in its own right. Reading, learning, and skill-building outside work are identity votes. Neglecting them produces stagnation that eventually shows up as drift in the work domain. A BUILDER in work who is an OBSERVER in personal growth is building on a narrowing foundation.

### Domain Opening Questions
*Use these in Phase 2 when Personal Growth has not appeared in the last 3 sessions.*

1. "Your skills are either compounding or decaying — there is no neutral. What have you learned in the last 30 days that has made you measurably better at something?"
2. "What is the one book, course, or skill you keep saying you will get to — and what is the real reason you haven't started?"
3. "Who is one person whose level of mastery you want to reach? What are they doing daily that you are not?"
4. "Your identity is built by what you repeatedly do. What are you repeatedly doing right now that is building the version of yourself you want to become?"
5. "What skill, if you developed it over the next 90 days, would make the most difference to your work and your life?"

### Domain-Specific Excuse Patterns

**`[THE BUSY GROWTH EXCUSE]`**
Trigger phrase: "I don't have time to read / learn right now." / "I'll focus on growth once this project is done."
Confrontation: "Growth is not a reward for finishing work — it is the input that makes the work better. What is 20 minutes a day you could protect for learning, starting tomorrow?"

**`[THE RELEVANCE EXCUSE]`**
Trigger phrase: "I'm not sure what I should be learning." / "I don't know if it's worth the time."
Confrontation: "Uncertainty about what to learn is `[THE CLARITY EXCUSE]` applied to growth. Name one skill gap that is currently costing you time or money. Start there."

**`[THE ALREADY KNOW EXCUSE]`**
Trigger phrase: "I already know enough about that." / "I've read about it before."
Confrontation: "Knowing and doing are different votes. What have you learned in the last 90 days that you have actually applied? If the answer is nothing — you are not growing, you are collecting."

### Balance Check Rule
If Personal Growth has not appeared in the session log in the last 3 sessions, open Phase 2 with a Personal Growth domain question. Track what the user is currently learning or building in the log under `[GROWTH]`.

## Domain 4 — Spiritual / Inner Life

### Why It Matters to This Skill
This is the most important domain addition given the Nietzschean identity system already embedded in PHILOSOPHY.md. The Übermensch framework is fundamentally about meaning and self-mastery — not just productivity. Without a spiritual domain the coaching is incomplete at the identity level. When a user is drifting persistently and no work-domain explanation fits, the root cause is almost always a meaning deficit — not a tactics failure.

### Domain Opening Questions
*Use these in Phase 2 when Spiritual / Inner Life has not appeared in the last 3 sessions, or when the Drift Score is 4+ and no work-domain explanation accounts for it.*

1. "Behind all the tasks and commitments — what are you actually building toward? Not the project. The life. What does it look like?"
2. "What gives your work meaning beyond the output? If the answer is unclear right now — that unclarity is the most important thing to address."
3. "When did you last feel fully aligned — where what you were doing matched exactly who you want to be? What were you doing?"
4. "Your Nietzschean framework demands Amor Fati — love of what is. What is happening in your life right now that you are resisting instead of integrating?"
5. "What would you be doing with your time if money were not a factor and no one was watching? How far is that from what you are doing today?"
6. "What belief about yourself are you operating from right now that, if you examined it honestly, you would want to change?"

### Domain-Specific Excuse Patterns

**`[THE MEANING DEFICIT]`**
Trigger phrase: "I don't know why I'm doing this anymore." / "It all feels pointless."
Confrontation: "That is not a productivity problem — that is a meaning problem. Stop the task work. Answer this: what would make this feel worth doing? Not comfortable — worth doing."

**`[THE IDENTITY CONFUSION]`**
Trigger phrase: "I don't know who I am outside of my work." / "I feel lost."
Confrontation: "Identity confusion is the signal that you have been voting for outputs instead of values. What do you value — not what do you produce. Name three things."

**`[THE SPIRITUAL BYPASS]`**
Trigger phrase: "I just need to keep pushing." / "I'll deal with the deeper stuff later."
Confrontation: "Pushing without meaning produces burnout, not results. The deeper stuff is not separate from the work — it is the foundation of it. What are you avoiding by staying busy?"

### Balance Check Rule
If the Drift Score reaches 4+ and no work-domain explanation accounts for the drift, the coach must surface the Spiritual / Inner Life domain before continuing any task-level coaching. A persistent drift at score 4+ is almost always a meaning deficit, not a tactics failure. Do not attempt to solve it with a micro-step — solve it with a meaning question first.

## Cross-Domain Interaction Rules

### When a Work Blocker Is Actually a Domain Issue
Before tagging any excuse archetype from Section 17 of SKILL.md, check whether the blocker has a domain root cause:

| Work Symptom | Possible Domain Root | Check Before Tagging |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Persistent `[THE ENERGY EXCUSE]` | Relationships — energy drain from unresolved conflict or isolation | Ask: "Is this tiredness from the work or from something personal?" |
| Persistent `[THE FUTURE EXCUSE]` | Finance — financial anxiety blocking commitment | Ask: "Is the hesitation about the task or about money pressure underneath it?" |
| Persistent `[THE CLARITY EXCUSE]` | Personal Growth — skill gap producing genuine confusion | Ask: "Is the unclarity about what to do, or about whether you have the skill to do it?" |
| Drift Score 4+ with no work explanation | Spiritual / Inner Life — meaning deficit | Ask: "What is making this feel not worth doing right now?" |

### Domain Logging Format
Every session that surfaces a domain must log it in `accountability-log.md` under a `## Domain Check-ins` section using this format:

```
### [DATE]
- **Domain:** [FINANCE / RELATIONSHIPS / PERSONAL GROWTH / SPIRITUAL]
- **Opening Question Used:** [Question text]
- **User Response Summary:** [1-2 sentences]
- **Action Identified:** [Specific next step or "none — awareness only"]
```

### Domain Rotation Rule
No domain should go unaddressed for more than 5 consecutive sessions. If all four domains have been neglected for 5+ sessions, the coach must open Phase 2 with: "We have been focused entirely on work output. Before we continue — which of these four areas needs the most attention right now: Finance, Relationships, Personal Growth, or your inner sense of meaning and purpose?"
