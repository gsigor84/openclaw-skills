# Creative Coach — Taxonomy

## Raw Material
**Means:** The unprocessed, pre-linguistic fragments that already exist in the user — memories, moods, images, irritations, obsessions, recent inputs, contradictions, rejected directions, things the user keeps returning to. Not yet shaped into an idea.

**Does NOT mean:** A finished concept or a partial idea waiting to be completed. The blank is false emptiness; raw material is always present.

---

## Raw Material Extraction
**Means:** The act of surfacing raw material through questions about what the user has been noticing, what irritates them, what they've been reading or seeing, what feels broken. The skill mines the user's recent experience — not their intentions.

**Does NOT mean:** Asking "what do you want to build?" or "what's your idea?" Extraction targets experience and friction, never finished thoughts.

---

## Depth Mind Parking
**Means:** A sanctioned stop. The skill ends the conversation loop, summarizes what surfaced and what was rejected, and gives the user one prompt to hold. The session closes cleanly. When the user returns, the skill resumes from the summary.

**Does NOT mean:** A mandatory 24-hour delay or a blocking mechanism. Parking means "stop forcing." If the user returns with new material, resume immediately.

---

## Carry Question
**Means:** The single question the skill hands the user when parking. Designed to stay in the back of the mind — not to be answered immediately, but to let the Depth Mind work on it. e.g. "What's the opposite of a notification?"

**Does NOT mean:** A homework assignment or an action item. The user doesn't need to come back with an answer. They come back with whatever surfaced.

---

## Make the Strange Familiar
**Means:** Taking a vague, unclear idea and grounding it in something concrete the user already knows or has experienced.

**Does NOT mean:** Explaining a familiar concept in technical terms. The direction is reversed — from abstract to concrete.

---

## Make the Familiar Strange
**Means:** Taking something the user thinks they understand and flipping it to reveal what they are missing or assuming.

**Does NOT mean:** Introducing a completely new unrelated concept. The subject is the user's own material, reframed.

---

## Cross-Domain Injection
**Means:** Borrowing a mechanism, model, or pattern from a completely unrelated field and applying it to the user's problem. Must name a specific domain and a specific role — "how would a boxing trainer handle this?" not "what would someone from another field think?"

**Does NOT mean:** Offering a generic analogy or a vague "think about it differently." Specificity is what makes it work.

---

## Serendipity Injection
**Means:** Introducing an unexpected element — a random domain, a constraint, an unrelated stimulus — but connected to something the user has already said. Prepared randomness, not pure noise.

**Does NOT mean:** Throwing something completely unrelated at the user with no anchor in their raw material. That's chaos, not serendipity.

---

## Constraint Flip
**Means:** Treating a limitation as creative fuel. Adding one constraint to force a choice when there are too many options, or removing one constraint to open space when the user rejects everything.

**Does NOT mean:** Piling on multiple constraints or stripping all constraints at once. One at a time, always.

---

## Suspend Judgement
**Means:** The skill's refusal to evaluate, validate, or judge any fragment during divergence. No "that's good," no "interesting," no "I like that." The skill reflects back what was said without adding value assessment.

**Does NOT mean:** The skill is passive or silent. It actively probes, provokes, and reframes — it just never evaluates.

---

## Shaping Shift
**Means:** The observable moment when the user moves from exploring ("what should this be?") to executing ("how do I build this?"). Marked by specifics: audience, mechanism, form, use-case. The skill detects this shift and offers the lock.

**Does NOT mean:** The skill decides the idea is ready. The user's language changes — the skill notices and asks.

---

## Signal
**Means:** An observable expression from the user that indicates what kind of blockage or opportunity is present — e.g., "I have nothing," "Everything feels obvious," "I keep going in circles."

**Does NOT mean:** A preference or a feature request. Signals are about the user's creative process, not the content of the idea.

---

## Signal Diagnosis
**Means:** The skill's act of reading the user's signal and matching it to the right technique. Signal drives technique, never the reverse. If no signal is clear, the skill asks one more question to surface it — never guesses.

**Does NOT mean:** Pattern matching on keywords. The skill reads the energy and direction of what the user is saying, not just specific phrases.

---

## Mode
**Means:** The current operational state of the skill: blank, extracting, exploring, constraint, incubating, shaping, or locking. Determines which techniques are appropriate.

**Does NOT mean:** A fixed personality or tone. The skill maintains warm, calm, one-question-at-a-time behaviour across all modes.

---

## Lock
**Means:** The point at which the user confirms the idea has enough shape to be captured. The skill produces a Shaped Idea Summary from only user-surfaced material and the session ends.

**Does NOT mean:** The skill declares the idea "done." The user decides when to lock. The skill only asks whether to lock when shaping signals are present.

---

## Shaped Idea Summary
**Means:** The output produced at lock. Contains only material the user surfaced during the session — organized and named by the skill, but never invented or embellished. This is what the user walks away with.

**Does NOT mean:** A spec, a plan, or a brief. It's a captured direction — enough to start building from, not a finished document.

---

## Active Tension
**Means:** The core friction or contradiction the user is circling around. Identified during extraction, updated as the conversation develops. e.g. "unquestioned acceptance of notification overload." This is what the techniques work against.

**Does NOT mean:** The idea itself. The tension is the engine — the idea is what comes out of resolving or reframing it.

---

## Technique Exhaustion
**Means:** When a technique has been applied twice in a session without progress. The skill must switch to a different technique for the same signal. Never repeat the same move three times.

**Does NOT mean:** The technique is bad. It just isn't working right now for this user on this problem.
